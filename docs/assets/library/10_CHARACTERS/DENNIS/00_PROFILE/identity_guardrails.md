# Identity Guardrails — Dennis

These rules define traits that must remain consistent when generating images of Dennis.

## Core Physical Identity

Always preserve:

- medium height around 175 cm (5 ft 9 in)
- soft_heavy build with a naturally untrained body
- hip-dominant frame with silhouette emphasis in the hips, glutes, and thighs
- moderate shoulders that read lighter than the lower body
- soft torso with natural stomach contour and minimal muscular definition
- thicker lower-body proportions, especially fuller hips, glutes, and thighs

Avoid:

- athletic V-shaped torsos or shoulder-dominant silhouettes
- narrow hips, flattened glutes, or leg-light proportions
- visible muscular definition, runner builds, or bodybuilder physiques
- elongated or slim silhouettes that remove his grounded lower-body mass

---

## Facial Identity

Always preserve:

- soft_round face shape
- soft_jawline with low angularity
- short dark brown hair with natural texture and parting
- light skin with freckles across the nose and cheeks
- rectangular eyeglasses
- calm, gentle, approachable facial presence

Avoid:

- sharp angular faces or strong square jawlines
- older or hardened-looking facial structure
- heavily styled or dramatic hair silhouettes
- facial hair unless explicitly required for a scene

---

## Style Identity

Always preserve:

- domestic_soft and soft_masculine aesthetic
- relaxed casual everyday clothing
- simple, understated silhouettes rather than sharp tailoring
- navy, neutral tones, and soft earth tones
- rectangular glasses as a recurring accessory

Avoid:

- sporty performance styling or gymwear-coded fashion
- high-fashion, sharply tailored, or aggressively masculine styling
- neon or highly saturated palettes
- elaborate accessories or statement-heavy styling

---

## Body Language

Typical movement:

- relaxed_natural movement with calm physical energy
- restrained gestures without sharp or athletic motion

Typical posture:

- relaxed_neutral stance with grounded weight distribution
- natural, modest posture rather than dominant or performative posing

Avoid:

- aggressive, dominant, or confrontational body language
- exaggerated athletic posing or confident-open hero stances
